## ❓ About Us

**Optim.Lab.** at the Department of Statistics and Data Science, University of Seoul, studies the fundamental principles of machine learning and develops optimization methodologies for learning models.

Our research aims to understand how learning algorithms operate, analyze their theoretical properties, and design effective optimization strategies for model training and generalization.

We primarily work with:

- 🌐 Network data  
- 🏆 Ranking data  
- 📈 Stock market and financial time-series data  

Based on these domains, we develop:

- Predictive models  
- Generative models  

---

## 🚀 Research Areas


### 🧬 AI for Knowledge Graph
We develop models that infer undiscovered relationships using the **CTD (Comparative Toxicogenomics Database)**, which summarizes causal relationships, correlations, and influence scores among **Chemicals, Genes, and Diseases**.  

Our goal is to design AI models capable of predicting previously unknown connections within biological knowledge graphs, supporting scientific discovery and environmental health research.

- Heterogeneous Graph Embedding  
- Link Prediction  


### 🔎 AI for Information Retrieval
We study **Information Retrieval (IR) for complex tasks**, focusing on improving retrieval effectiveness in dynamic and high-dimensional environments.  

Our research integrates causal reasoning and learning-based recommendation systems to enhance ranking performance and query efficiency.

- Causal Inference  
- Recommendation Algorithms  
- Query Performance Prediction  

### 🧠 AI for Synthetic Data Generation
We investigate methods for **multimodal synthetic data generation**, developing generative AI models capable of producing high-quality artificial data across diverse modalities.  

Our research contributes to privacy-preserving AI and data-efficient machine learning.

- Variational AutoEncoder (VAE)  
- Generative Models  

### 💰 Financial AI
We conduct research on:
- Reinforcement learning-based automated trading agents  
- Multivariate financial time-series forecasting models  

Our goal is to build intelligent financial systems that combine representation learning, sequential modeling, and decision optimization.

- Reinforcement Learning  
- Graph Embedding  
- Causal Inference  
- Transformer Models
